Ofada Stew Recipe

Description

Ofada Stew, also known as Ayamase, is a rich and spicy Nigerian stew made from green bell peppers, onions, and a blend of indigenous spices. This stew is commonly served with Ofada rice, a locally grown variety with a unique aroma. Traditionally prepared with bleached palm oil, it features assorted meats such as beef, tripe, and offals, giving it a deep, smoky taste. The secret to its rich flavor lies in slow cooking, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully.

Ingredients

  • 6 green bell peppers
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust for spice level)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 cup assorted meats (beef, tripe, kidney, liver, etc.)
  • 1 cup palm oil (bleached for 10 minutes)
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon locust beans (iru)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 seasoning cube
  • 2 boiled eggs (optional, for garnish)
  1. Blend the green bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and onions into a rough paste.
  2. Bleach the palm oil by heating it on low for about 10 minutes until it turns golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped onions and locust beans, frying until fragrant.
  4. Pour in the blended pepper mixture and allow it to cook until the oil separates.
  5. Season with ground crayfish, salt, black pepper, white pepper, and seasoning cube.
  6. Add the assorted meats and stir well, allowing the flavors to blend.
  7. Simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Add boiled eggs for garnish and let them absorb the flavors for a few minutes.
  9. Serve hot with Ofada rice.
