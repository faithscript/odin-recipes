Asun Recipe

Description

Asun is a spicy Nigerian dish made from grilled goat meat tossed in a fiery pepper sauce. Originally from the Yoruba-speaking regions, this dish is known for its smoky and intensely spicy flavor. The goat meat is first grilled until tender, then stir-fried with onions, scotch bonnet peppers, and other seasonings. It is a popular delicacy at parties and gatherings, often served as an appetizer or side dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg goat meat (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable or palm oil
  • 5 scotch bonnet peppers (blended or finely chopped)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon seasoning cube
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (for extra smoky flavor)
  1. Season the goat meat with salt, thyme, black pepper, and seasoning cube.
  2. Grill the meat over an open flame or in an oven at 375°F (190°C) until it is tender and slightly charred.
  3. In a pan, heat the vegetable or palm oil and sauté the onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  4. Add the blended or chopped scotch bonnet peppers and fry for 3-5 minutes.
  5. Stir in the smoked paprika and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. Add the grilled goat meat and mix well, allowing the flavors to infuse.
  7. Cook on low heat for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Serve hot as a party snack or side dish.
